The WEF released "Making Agentic AI Work for Government — A Readiness Framework" in April. It's the cleanest conceptual model out there. But it's a PDF.

So I built a working visualisation: agenticgov.vercel.app 🇵🇰

Six interconnected agentic apps (Mission Control, TenderAI, FiscalAI, ReadinessMap, GovBench, TransparencyAI) covering 70 government functions, with live cross-app flows. Singapore is the primary reference jurisdiction.

I'm sharing this because Pakistan's medium-term planning conversation needs a credible AI-in-government story, and abstract frameworks don't move that conversation. A working artefact does. The architecture transfers cleanly — the question is which functions you'd prioritise first given Planning Commission and NCAI's direction of travel.

There's a live scan demo on the homepage that walks through one end-to-end flow.

Would value reactions from anyone working in or adjacent to the Planning Commission / Cabinet Division / NCAI conversation. Happy to walk through it.
