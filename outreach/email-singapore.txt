The WEF released "Making Agentic AI Work for Government — A Readiness Framework" last month. Strong report, but mostly conceptual. I wanted to see what it would actually look like running in Singapore, so I built it.

agenticgov.vercel.app

It's a working visualisation of six interconnected agentic apps — Mission Control, TenderAI, FiscalAI, ReadinessMap, GovBench, TransparencyAI — covering 70 government functions. Real SG agencies are wired in: GovTech, IMDA, MAS, MOH, MOE, MTI, MND, MOM, MSF, PSD. Real programmes too: SkillsFuture, KidSTART, Smart Nation 2030, Singpass, MyInfo. You'll recognise everything.

The interesting part isn't any single app. It's the cross-app flows — how a tender anomaly in TenderAI propagates to FiscalAI, surfaces in Mission Control, and ends up scored on GovBench. That's where the readiness framework actually gets operationalised.

If you have 5 mins, here's what to look at first: hit the homepage, run the live scan demo, then click into Mission Control to see one flow end-to-end. About 3 minutes of clicking gives you the architecture.

I built this as a thinking artefact, not a pitch. But I'd genuinely value your reaction — whether it rings true against what you're seeing inside government, where it underweights reality, what's missing.
