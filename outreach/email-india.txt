The WEF released "Making Agentic AI Work for Government — A Readiness Framework" in April. It's the cleanest conceptual model I've seen on agentic AI in the public sector, but it stops short of showing what the architecture looks like running.

So I built a working visualisation: agenticgov.vercel.app

Six interconnected agentic apps — Mission Control, TenderAI, FiscalAI, ReadinessMap, GovBench, TransparencyAI — covering 70 government functions, with live cross-app flows. Singapore is the primary reference jurisdiction in the demo (real agencies and programmes wired in), but the architecture is jurisdiction-agnostic.

I think this is the closest visual to what the WEF report's recommendations would look like in an IndiaStack context. The same data-rails-plus-agentic-orchestration logic that already underpins Aadhaar, DigiLocker, and UPI — applied across NITI Aayog priority sectors and Cabinet Secretariat coordination layers.

If you have 5 mins, start on the homepage, run the live scan demo, then open Mission Control. The cross-app flow from TenderAI to FiscalAI to GovBench is the bit worth tracing — that's where the architecture either holds together or falls apart.

I'd value your reaction more than most people's. Where does this read true against IndiaStack reality? Where does it miss? What would the NITI / DigiLocker / Cabinet Secretariat version of this prioritise first?
