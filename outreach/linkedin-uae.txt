The WEF released its agentic AI readiness framework for governments last month. Clean conceptual model, but the report stops short of showing what it looks like running.

So I built it: agenticgov.vercel.app 🇦🇪

Six interconnected agentic apps — Mission Control, TenderAI, FiscalAI, ReadinessMap, GovBench, TransparencyAI — covering 70 government functions. Singapore is the reference jurisdiction, but the architecture is the point.

The UAE is already further along on this than most governments I talk to. So the question I'd really value reactions to isn't "is this useful" — it's whether the architecture rings true against what you're actually building. Where does it underweight reality? What would the MoCA / federal-ministry version of this look like?

There's a live scan demo on the homepage that traces one flow across all six apps.
